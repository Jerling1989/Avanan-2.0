Avanan Anti-Phishing





Stop Threats Before They Hit Your Inbox

Deploy in minutes

Deploying Avanan is as easy as authorizing an application—no need to install a proxy or agent. Instantly, Avanan starts protecting your organization from incoming phishing attacks and scanning for existing threats in your inbox.

Catch more attacks

Avanan acts as an additional layer of security that runs after Microsoft and Google’s default email scan, but before the email hits the user’s inbox. As a result, our unique machine learning algorithm is optimized to catch attacks that bypass other email security tools.

Catch historical and internal threats

Avanan’s anti-phishing scans historical emails and monitors activity in your organization across your SaaS applications to determine prior trust relations between senders and receivers. It uses this data to detect anomalous behaviour and compromised accounts that wouldn’t be flagged by an ordinary DKIM, SPF, or DMARC check.

Invisible to hackers

Other security solutions require you to change your MX records, which broadcasts what security you are using to the world, allowing hackers to design attacks specifically to bypass your protections. Avanan does not change your MX records, making it impossible for hackers to see what security you are using and test their attacks against it.

Protect more than just email

Phishing on the cloud does not always come through your email. There has been an increase in phishing attacks that make use of file sharing and instant messaging applications. Avanan connects to all of your cloud applications, and scans every file and message using the same technology we use for your email, so we can block phishing attacks no matter the entry point.

In addition to looking for entry points, our unmatched visibility allows us to detect compromised accounts by pinpointing suspicious behavior across SaaS applications. When a breach is detected, Avanan can take action and revoke the users access to every application.

Avanan Cloud Security.

Avanan implements the traditional email security layers of SPF, DKIM and DMARC checks, as well as URL filtering by leveraging three major data sources for URL blacklists. But these methods are generally already implemented by the default security and only catch about 15% of phishing attacks at the time the email is received. Most phishing attacks that bypass the default security are true zero-day and come from legitimate senders.
